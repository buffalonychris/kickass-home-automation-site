What is Home Automation?

Devices sensing the world, deciding with rules, and acting—without you having to think about it.

Core building blocks

  • Sensors — motion, contact, water leak, temperature, humidity, CO/CO₂, light (lux).
  • Actuators — switches/dimmers, outlets, valves, blinds/curtains, thermostats, sirens.
  • Hubs — Alexa/Google (cloud‑first), or Home Assistant (local‑first, highly flexible).
  • Protocols — Wi‑Fi, Zigbee, Z‑Wave, Thread/Matter; each has pros/cons.

Why it matters

Automations reduce friction: lights stay on when you’re present, shut when you leave; leaks are caught in minutes, not hours; fans respond to humidity; coffee tells the house it’s time to wake up.
